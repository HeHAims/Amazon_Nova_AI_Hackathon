About Symbiotic AI

Governance Before Generation.

Our Mission

Symbiotic AI exists to build deterministic, auditable governance infrastructure for artificial intelligence systems. We believe AI safety should not rely on post-hoc moderation. Governance must precede generation.

What We Build

Our core framework, CMIS (Cognitive Mathematical Intelligence System), is a structured reasoning layer that evaluates intent, scope, risk, and constraints before any generative model produces output.

CMIS enforces:

  • Deterministic decision pathways
  • Explicit refusal handling
  • Bounded confidence scoring
  • Traceable governance envelopes
  • Reproducible reasoning states

Why Governance Matters

As AI adoption expands into regulated environments - education, healthcare, public institutions, and enterprise systems - explainability alone is insufficient.

Systems must demonstrate structured constraint, refusal capability, and audit-ready decision logic. CMIS is designed to serve as a governance firewall between human intent and generative capability.

Research Foundation

The CMIS framework integrates symbolic reasoning, deterministic classification logic, bias detection, contextual adequacy evaluation, and constitutional gating.

Development has included:

  • Governance benchmarking experiments
  • Determinism validation runs
  • Structured evidence bundling for audit review
  • School pilot deployment architecture planning
  • NSF SBIR Phase I proposal structuring

Founder

Hilarion Hernandez Alavez is the Founder and Lead Architect of Symbiotic AI. His work focuses on governance-first AI systems, symbolic reasoning frameworks, deterministic logic design, and alignment infrastructure for high-trust environments.

Rather than optimizing models for scale alone, his research centers on structural accountability - ensuring AI systems operate within declared, testable, and enforceable boundaries.
